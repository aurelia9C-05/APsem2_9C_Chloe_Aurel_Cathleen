Matter and Atom

A. Solid, Liquid, and Gas:

  • Solid: A solid is a state of matter where particles are tightly packed together, giving it a fixed shape and volume.
  • Liquid: A liquid is a state of matter where particles are close together but can move freely, allowing it to flow and take the shape of its container.
  • Gas: A gas is a state of matter where particles are widely spaced and move freely, allowing it to expand to fill any container.
  • Solid -> Liquid (Melting)
  • Liquid -> Solid (Freezing)
  • Liquid -> Gas (Evaporation)
  • Solid -> Gas (Sublimation)
  • Gas -> Solid (Deposition)
  • Gas -> Liquid (Condensation)

    • B. Atoms:

    • Structure of Atom:
    • Proton (found in the nucleus): Positive Charge and cannot move
    • Neutron (found in the nucleus): Neutral Charge and cannot move
    • Electrons (found in the orbits): Negative Charge and can move
    • Orbits: Paths that electrons follow around the nucleus.
    • Symbol of an Atom: Depends on the element (e.g., O for oxygen, Au for gold).
    • Isotopes: Atoms of the same element with the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons.
    • Atoms, Molecules, Compounds, and Ions:
    • Atoms are the basic building blocks of matter.
    • Molecules are groups of two or more atoms.
    • Compounds are a group of two or more molecules.
    • Ions are atoms or molecules that have gained or lost electrons.


    C. Periodic Table:

    • Period/Row: A horizontal row of elements containing the number of electron shells.
    • Group/Column: A vertical column of elements containing the number of outer electrons.

      • Why are There a lot of Numbers in 1 Box of Elements?
    Atomic Number: Number of Protons
    Atomic Mass: Protons + Neutrons
    Charge: Protons - Electrons
    Nucleus: Protons + Neutrons
    Proton > Neutron: Positive Charge
    Proton > Neutron: Negative Charge


    D. Elements, Compounds, and Mixtures:

    • Elements are simple substances made of one type of atom.
    • Compounds are substances made when two or more different elements chemically combine.
    • Mixtures are combinations of two or more substances that are not chemically bonded.


    E. Changing Matter:

    • Physical Reaction: A change that does not alter the chemical composition.
    • Chemical Reaction: A process where substances change into new substances with different properties.
